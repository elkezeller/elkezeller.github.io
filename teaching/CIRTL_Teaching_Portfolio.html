I’m curretly enroled in the University of Arizona’s CIRTL Postdoc Pathway Program. This part of my website will evolve while I’m developing my teaching skills.

Welcome to my CIRTL teaching portfolio. This page contains a compilation of the evidence-based teaching materials required to complete the University of Arizona's CIRTL Postdoc Pathway Program. This two-semester postdoctoral CIRTL program combines training in theory and pedagogy of evidence-based teaching with an intensive, short-term co-teaching experience. In the first semester I completed the 8-week online CIRTL course, "An Introduction to Evidence-Based Undergraduate STEM Teaching" while meeting weekly with my cohort to discuss the course contents. The second semester is dedicated to applying the acuared skills through an intensive, short-term co-teaching partnership, equivalent to one unit or module of a class.

My goals for the program are to...

  • learn and apply strategies in active learning and develop practical skills in interacting with students and to understand the appropriate teaching techniques for diverse educational contexts, such as large undergraduate courses versus graduate-level seminars.
  • gain experience in developing classroom content, specifically learning how to anticipate the gaps in student knowledge and create classroom educational experiences to address those gaps.
  • develop a teaching curriculum that is rooted in an evidence-based teaching foundation and that I can build upon in my further career.
